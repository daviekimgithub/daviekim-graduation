Timeline

Education Timeline

Nursery School

Madam Mary, Madam Milka

Life felt extra special at some point when my mum was my teacher, making school feel like home. I remember acing the class one interview, making it into the top 10.

Class 1

Mr. Gakua

This was the first year without my mum around in school. My best friend, Kariuki, became my world. I remember feeling left out one day and crying all the way home because Kariuki and Bernard ran home without me.

My teacher was patient and understanding, especially when I would sneak off to play with my older brother Mwangi. One day I hid behind a mud kitchen but returned to class, where Mr. Gakua kindly didn’t question me.

Classes 1 to 3

Mr. Gakua

I was slow, often missing breaks as I was the last to finish. Despite this, my answers were always correct. Kariuki was a loyal friend, always standing up for me all through this time.

Class 4

Multiple Teachers

This was a big transition as I moved to having multiple teachers. I recall a day when I got sick and lay down under a tree during lunch. Faint voices calling, “Mwangi's brother has fainted,” led teachers to help me. From there to our headteacher’s car, nicknamed “Boflo.” I was taken to the hospital and soon recovered.

Classes 5 and 6

Realizing My Potential

Did I have any pontential for better academics? Yes I had! With influences from teachers like Mr. Kahenya, Mr. Githinji, Madam Monica, and Madam Mary, I grew more serious about my studies. I topped the class, holding my position throughout primary school. I even dreamed of becoming a neurosurgeon.

Classes 7 and 8

Mission In Action

My mission in life was in action! Attending Mission in Action school, I studied music and computer, unique subjects in Primary schools by then in 8-4-4 curriculum but these two impacted my life. The school also provided meals, no packed food anymore! Madam Momento helped me improve my handwriting, and Mr. Mdoe helped me with pronunciation. Although challenging with the 5 km daily walk, I sometimes spent my bus fare on mandazi.

KCPE 2014

Score: 318

It felt like a terrible failure, so I decided to repeat, aiming for a national school which required over 400 marks.

2015 - Second Attempt at Class 8

Result: 359

I felt I had truly done my best although I had not hit my target.

Secondary School

Admission Day

In Form 1, I competed with Salim, it was tough to top in the class. By Form 2, with Salim gone, I held the top position. Form 3, I was the deputy CU chairperson, and by Form 4, I was CU chair. Despite aiming for an A, I ended up with B plain.

Career Choice

Career Choice

All my career life was depending on this time. After much research, I applied for computer science at the University of Eldoret, this was the very first career among the choices I took and that what God had planned. Thankfully I went into an IT path.

University

University of Eldoret

The University of Eldoret was my first true step into adulthood. I made lifelong friends—Eric, Eugene, Innocent, Kevin—who encouraged and supported me. Reflecting on university, it’s a cherished chapter of growth. Here was freedom. Whether you attend classes or not, whether you do your exams or not, whether you study or not, no one bother other than you yourself. Maths, Physics, Algorithms, Programming, Data Structures, Quantum Computing, that's how hard campus life was!

Looking Back

Looking back at Kenya’s 8-4-4 curriculum, I’m grateful for the journey and the lessons. I thank God for His grace, my parents for their guidance, and pastors Brother Ngige, Brother Mburu, and Brother Githua for their spiritual mentorship. My brothers and sisters for standing with me, calls after calls they made. Career mentorship from Mr Elisha. My church friends never let me lost out in the world. My roomates for the life we shared.
